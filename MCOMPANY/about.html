    2. Dry Cleaning 🧼👕

    How Dry cleaning works:


    1️⃣ Pre-Treatment – Stains are identified and treated with special stain removers.
    2️⃣ Cleaning with Solvents – Clothes are placed in a machine that cleans them using a liquid solvent (usually perchloroethylene or hydrocarbon-based solvents) instead of water.
    3️⃣ Extraction & Drying – The solvent is removed, and the fabric is gently dried.
    4️⃣ Pressing & Finishing – Garments are steamed, pressed, and made ready for use.

    3. Shampooing

    How Shampooing Works:


    1️⃣ Pre-Treatment – Stains and heavily soiled areas are treated with a cleaning solution.
    2️⃣ Application – A foamy shampoo detergent is applied to the surface.
    3️⃣ Agitation – A brush or rotary machine scrubs the shampoo deep into the fibers to break down dirt.
    4️⃣ Extraction – A vacuum or wet extractor removes the foam along with dirt and grime.
    5️⃣ Drying – The surface is left to air dry or speed-dried with fans.

    Best Uses for Shampooing:


    ✅ Carpets & Rugs – Removes deep-seated dirt and refreshes fibers.
    ✅ Upholstery (Sofas & Chairs) – Lifts stains and restores fabric freshness.
    ✅ Mattresses – Eliminates dust, sweat stains, and allergens.
    ✅ Car Interiors (Seats & Floor Mats) – Cleans fabric seats and removes odors.

    Pros & Cons of Shampooing🧼🛋️:


    ✅ Pros:
    ✔ Deep Cleaning Power – Effectively removes dirt, stains, and odors.
    ✔ Restores Fabric Appearance – Brightens colors and refreshes the look.
    ✔ Affordable & Widely Available – Common in carpet and upholstery cleaning services.

    7. Post-Construction Cleaning 🏗️🧹

    Phases of Post-Construction Cleaning

    1. Rough Cleaning (Initial Phase) 🚧


    🔹 Done before flooring, painting, or finishing touches are completed.
    🔹 Involves removing large debris, construction dust, and leftover materials.
    ✔ Sweeping and vacuuming major dust.
    ✔ Removing paint and adhesive residues.
    ✔ Cleaning walls, windows, and doors of excess dust.

    2. Final / Deep Cleaning (After Construction is Completed) 🏡


    🔹 Focuses on detailed cleaning of all surfaces.
    ✔ Dusting and wiping down walls, ceilings, and fixtures.
    ✔ Vacuuming and mopping floors, carpets, and tiles.
    ✔ Sanitizing kitchens, bathrooms, and high-touch areas.
    ✔ Cleaning air vents, cabinets, and light switches.
    ✔ Washing windows, mirrors, and glass panels.

    3. Touch-Up Cleaning (Last Phase) ✨


    🔹 Performed a few days later to remove any settled dust.
    ✔ Re-cleaning windows and mirrors for a spotless finish.
    ✔ Checking corners, trims, and fixtures for dust or smudges.
    ✔ Final floor polishing or waxing (if needed).

    Best Cleaning Methods for Post-Construction Cleaning:


    🟢 HEPA Vacuuming – Removes fine dust and construction debris.
    🟢 Pressure Washing – Cleans driveways, sidewalks, and exteriors.
    🟢 Steam Cleaning – Disinfects and deep-cleans surfaces.
    🟢 Chemical Cleaning – Removes stubborn paint, glue, and cement stains.

    Who Needs Post-Construction Cleaning?


    🏠 Homeowners – After home renovations or new builds.
    🏢 Businesses & Offices – Preparing a newly built or renovated workspace.
    🏗️ Construction Companies – Ensuring a finished project is move-in ready.

    Why Post-Construction Cleaning is Important:


    ✅ Removes Hazardous Dust – Construction dust can cause breathing issues.
    ✅ Enhances Aesthetic Appeal – Makes the space look polished and brand new.
    ✅ Ensures Safety – Eliminates sharp debris and leftover construction materials.
    ✅ Speeds Up Move-In Process – Makes the space ready for use faster.
